# Administrative Law — French Administrative Law

- Code de justice administrative (CJA) — arts. L1, L211-1, L311-1, L521-1, L521-2, L911-1, R421-1, R421-5, L761-1 and following
- Code des relations entre le public et l'administration (CRPA) — arts. L100-1, L211-1, L211-2, L231-1, L311-1, L311-5, L311-6, L411-1 and following
- Code général des collectivités territoriales (CGCT) — arts. L2121-1, L2122-1, L2212-1, L2212-2, L2213-1, L3211-1, L4221-1 and following
- Code de l'urbanisme (CU) — arts. L421-1, L421-2, L421-3, L422-1, L424-1, L600-1 and following

## Key Articles

### CJA — Compétence et organisation (L.1+)

#### CJA Article L1 — Compétence de droit commun des tribunaux administratifs
> « Les tribunaux administratifs sont, en premier ressort et sous réserve des compétences attribuées aux autres juridictions administratives, juges de droit commun du contentieux administratif. »
Source: Legifrance
Keywords: compétence, droit commun, premier ressort, contentieux administratif

#### CJA Article L2 — Conseil d'État, juge de cassation
> « Le Conseil d'État statue souverainement sur les recours en cassation dirigés contre les décisions rendues en dernier ressort par les juridictions administratives. »
Source: Legifrance
Keywords: cassation, Conseil d'État, dernier ressort

#### CJA Article L3 — Conseil d'État, juge de premier et dernier ressort
> « Le Conseil d'État connaît en premier et dernier ressort [...] des recours dirigés contre les ordonnances du Président de la République et les décrets, des litiges relatifs à la situation individuelle des fonctionnaires nommés par décret du Président de la République [...]. »
Source: Legifrance (résumé des principales compétences)
Keywords: premier et dernier ressort, décrets, ordonnances, fonctionnaires

#### CJA Article L7 — Formation collégiale
> « Un tribunal administratif ou une cour administrative d'appel statue en formation collégiale, sauf exceptions prévues par le présent code. »

Keywords: formation collégiale, juge unique, exceptions

### CJA — Référés (L.521-1+)

#### CJA Article L521-1 — Référé-suspension
> « Quand une décision administrative, même de rejet, fait l'objet d'une requête en annulation ou en réformation, le juge des référés, saisi d'une demande en ce sens, peut ordonner la suspension de l'exécution de cette décision, ou de certains de ses effets, lorsque l'urgence le justifie et qu'il est fait état d'un moyen propre à créer, en l'état de l'instruction, un doute sérieux quant à la légalité de la décision. »
Source: Legifrance
Keywords: référé-suspension, urgence, doute sérieux, suspension

#### CJA Article L521-2 — Référé-liberté
> « Saisi d'une demande en ce sens justifiée par l'urgence, le juge des référés peut ordonner toutes mesures nécessaires à la sauvegarde d'une liberté fondamentale à laquelle une personne morale de droit public ou un organisme de droit privé chargé de la gestion d'un service public aurait porté, dans l'exercice d'un de ses pouvoirs, une atteinte grave et manifestement illégale. »
Source: Legifrance
Keywords: référé-liberté, liberté fondamentale, atteinte grave, 48 heures

#### CJA Article L521-3 — Référé mesures utiles
> « En cas d'urgence et sur simple requête qui sera recevable même en l'absence de décision administrative préalable, le juge des référés peut ordonner toutes autres mesures utiles sans faire obstacle à l'exécution d'aucune décision administrative. »
Source: Legifrance
Keywords: référé mesures utiles, urgence, mesures conservatoires

#### CJA Article L522-1 — Procédure de référé
> « Le juge des référés statue au terme d'une procédure contradictoire écrite ou orale. Lorsqu'il lui est demandé de prononcer les mesures visées aux articles L521-1 et L521-2, de prendre les mesures conservatoires visées à l'article L521-3, il informe les parties de la date et de l'heure de l'audience publique. »
Source: Legifrance
Keywords: procédure contradictoire, audience publique, délai

### CJA — Recours et délais (R.421-1+)

#### CJA Article R421-1 — Délai de recours
> « La juridiction ne peut être saisie que par voie de recours formé contre une décision, et ce, dans les deux mois à partir de la notification ou de la publication de la décision attaquée. »
Source: Legifrance
Keywords: délai deux mois, notification, publication, recevabilité

#### CJA Article R421-2 — Décision implicite de rejet
> « Sauf disposition législative ou réglementaire contraire, dans les cas où le silence gardé par l'autorité administrative sur une demande vaut décision de rejet, les intéressés disposent, pour former un recours, d'un délai de deux mois à compter de la date à laquelle est née la décision implicite de rejet. »
Source: Legifrance
Keywords: silence, rejet implicite, délai, recours

#### CJA Article R421-5 — Opposabilité des délais
> « Les délais de recours contre une décision administrative ne sont opposables qu'à la condition d'avoir été mentionnés, ainsi que les voies de recours, dans la notification de la décision. »
Source: Legifrance
Keywords: mention voies et délais, notification, opposabilité

### CJA — Injonctions (L.911-1+)

#### CJA Article L911-1 — Injonction d'agir
> « Lorsque sa décision implique nécessairement qu'une personne morale de droit public ou un organisme de droit privé chargé de la gestion d'un service public prenne une mesure d'exécution dans un sens déterminé, la juridiction, saisie de conclusions en ce sens, prescrit, par la même décision, cette mesure assortie, le cas échéant, d'un délai d'exécution. »
Source: Legifrance
Keywords: injonction, mesure d'exécution, sens déterminé

#### CJA Article L911-2 — Injonction de réexamen
> « Lorsque sa décision implique nécessairement qu'une personne morale de droit public ou un organisme de droit privé chargé de la gestion d'un service public prenne à nouveau une décision après une nouvelle instruction, la juridiction, saisie de conclusions en ce sens, prescrit, par la même décision, que cette nouvelle décision doit intervenir dans un délai déterminé. »
Source: Legifrance
Keywords: réexamen, nouvelle instruction, délai

#### CJA Article L911-3 — Astreinte
> « La juridiction peut assortir, dans la même décision, l'injonction prescrite en application des articles L911-1 et L911-2 d'une astreinte. »
Source: Legifrance
Keywords: astreinte, exécution, contrainte financière

#### CJA Article L911-4 — Exécution des décisions
> « En cas d'inexécution d'une décision rendue par une juridiction administrative, la partie intéressée peut demander au tribunal administratif ou à la cour administrative d'appel qui a rendu la décision d'en assurer l'exécution. »
Source: Legifrance
Keywords: inexécution, exécution forcée, demande d'exécution

### CJA — Frais de justice

#### CJA Article L761-1 — Frais irrépétibles

## Core Principles

- **Séparation des ordres de juridiction (separation of administrative and judicial orders)**: French law maintains a strict dual-court system — civil/criminal courts under the Cour de cassation, administrative courts under the Conseil d'État. The Tribunal des conflits resolves jurisdictional conflicts. Founded by the Blanco decision (TC, 8 févr. 1873).

- **Acte administratif unilatéral (unilateral administrative act)**: The administration may unilaterally create, modify, or extinguish legal situations without the consent of those affected. Such acts benefit from a presumption of legality and are immediately enforceable (exécutoire). Core to CJA jurisdiction.

- **Service public (public service)**: State activities organized to satisfy a collective need under government control, subject to special legal rules (continuity, equality, adaptability — the lois de Rolland). Determining whether an activity is a public service is a key gateway to administrative jurisdiction.

- **Responsabilité administrative (administrative liability)**: The state may be held liable for unlawful acts (faute de service) or, exceptionally, for lawful acts causing special and abnormal harm (responsabilité sans faute). Governed entirely by administrative law since Blanco (1873).

- **Recours pour excès de pouvoir (excess of power — REP)**: The primary tool for challenging administrative decisions. An applicant may invoke grounds of illegality (illégalité externe: incompetence, vice de forme; illégalité interne: détournement de pouvoir, violation de la loi, erreur manifeste d'appréciation). Governed by CJA L1 and R421-1 onwards.

## Landmark Decisions

### Blanco — Tribunal des conflits, 8 février 1873, n° 00012
- **Facts:** A child named Agnès Blanco was injured by a wagon operated by a State tobacco factory in Bordeaux. Her father sued the State before civil courts for compensation.
- **Rule:** Liability of the State for damage caused by public servants in the operation of a public service is governed neither by the Civil Code nor by general principles of civil law; it has its own special rules and falls within administrative jurisdiction.
- **Significance:** The founding text of French administrative law. It established that the State is subject to its own legal order (droit administratif) distinct from private law, and that administrative courts — not civil courts — have jurisdiction over State liability claims.

### Arrêt Cadot — Conseil d'État, 13 décembre 1889, n° 66145
- **Facts:** An engineer dismissed from the city of Marseille brought his damages claim directly before the Conseil d'État after the city council rejected his petition.
- **Rule:** The Conseil d'État recognized its own general jurisdiction over administrative disputes, abandoning the earlier doctrine of "ministre-juge" (under which ministers had first-instance jurisdiction).
- **Significance:** Established the Conseil d'État as the general judge of administrative litigation (juge de droit commun), a role later delegated to tribunaux administratifs in 1953. Marks the birth of the modern administrative court system.

### Dehaene — Conseil d'État Assemblée, 7 juillet 1950, n° 01645
- **Facts:** Civil servants at the Préfecture du Rhône went on strike; the prefect dismissed some of them. The dismissed employees challenged the dismissals, arguing a constitutional right to strike.
- **Rule:** While the right to strike has constitutional value (Preamble of 1946), it can be restricted for civil servants in the interest of continuity of public service. In the absence of legislation, the head of government may regulate its exercise.
- **Significance:** Confirmed that constitutional rights are not absolute for public servants; established the "nécessaire conciliation" between the right to strike and continuity of public service — a balance still applied today.

### Barel — Conseil d'État Assemblée, 28 mai 1954, n° 28117
- **Facts:** Several candidates were refused admission to the concours for the École Nationale d'Administration (ENA) without explanation. One of them was suspected of Communist Party membership.
- **Rule:** The principle of equal access to public employment prohibits administrative authorities from rejecting candidates on grounds of their political opinions. The CE ordered the administration to produce the files, reversing the burden of proof.
- **Significance:** Landmark ruling on equal access to public service (égale admissibilité aux emplois publics, Art. 6 DDHC) and on procedural equality. Also notable as an early example of the CE compelling the administration to disclose documents.

### Canal — Conseil d'État Assemblée, 19 octobre 1962, n° 58502
- **Facts:** Three OAS leaders sentenced to death by a special military court (Cour militaire de justice) created by presidential ordonnance challenged the legality of the court.
- **Rule:** Ordonnances issued under Article 16 of the Constitution (emergency powers) that permanently modify the legal order are justiciable acts subject to review by the Conseil d'État.
- **Significance:** The CE asserted its power of review over acts taken under the most extreme executive power, rejecting the notion of "actes de gouvernement" for Article 16 ordonnances affecting fundamental rights. A critical check on executive authority.

### Nicolo — Conseil d'État Assemblée, 20 octobre 1989, n° 108243
- **Facts:** A French citizen challenged the validity of elections to the European Parliament, arguing that participation of overseas territories violated an earlier French electoral law.
- **Rule:** The Conseil d'État accepted, for the first time, to review the compatibility of French statutes with international treaties (here, the EEC Treaty). A French law that is inconsistent with a later treaty must be set aside.
- **Significance:** Ended the "loi-écran" doctrine under which a statute shielded from treaty review. Aligned French administrative law with the constitutional requirement of Art. 55 of the Constitution. The CE joined civil courts and the Constitutional Council in accepting the primacy of EU law.

### Danthony — Conseil d'État Assemblée, 23 décembre 2011, n° 335033
- **Facts:** Public servants challenged a decree reorganizing the administration, arguing that a consultative committee had not been properly consulted before its adoption.
- **Rule:** A procedural irregularity (vice de procédure) does not necessarily lead to the annulment of the administrative decision. Annulment is warranted only if the irregularity (1) was liable to influence the decision (a exercé une influence sur le sens de la décision) or (2) deprived the claimants of a procedural guarantee.
- **Significance:** Major shift in administrative law. Before Danthony, virtually any procedural flaw could lead to annulment. The decision introduced a proportionate approach: minor procedural irregularities that could not have changed the outcome are tolerated. Now codified in many regulatory impact analyses and widely cited by lower courts.

### GISTI — Conseil d'État Assemblée, 12 juin 2020, n° 418142 (« lignes directrices »)
- **Facts:** The GISTI challenged guidelines issued by the Minister of the Interior on residence permits.
- **Rule:** Administrative guidelines (lignes directrices) producing legal effects may be challenged by REP, provided they are not merely internal instructions.
- **Significance:** Extended reviewable acts to soft-law instruments with normative effects. Builds on CE Ass., 21 mars 2016, Fairvesta, n° 368082, and CE Ass., 21 mars 2016, NC Numericable, n° 390023.

### Département de Tarn-et-Garonne — Conseil d'État Assemblée, 4 avril 2014, n° 358994
- **Facts:** The département challenged a public contract (marché public) of the commune de Montauban, arguing procurement rule violations. The département was a third party to the contract.
- **Rule:** Any third party with sufficiently direct and certain interests harmed by a public contract may bring a full-jurisdiction action (recours de plein contentieux). The judge weighs the gravity of the illegality against the public interest in contract stability.
- **Significance:** Overruled the Martin doctrine (CE, 1905) under which third parties could only challenge detachable acts by REP. Opened direct challenge of contracts through balanced full-jurisdiction review.

### Czabaj — Conseil d'État Assemblée, 13 juillet 2016, n° 387763
- **Facts:** A retired military officer challenged a pension decision notified without mention of time limits or remedies — under R421-5, the two-month period had not started running.
- **Rule:** Even absent proper notification of remedies, the recipient cannot challenge indefinitely. A reasonable time limit of one year from notification applies, except in exceptional circumstances.
- **Significance:** Created the "délai raisonnable" of one year as a backstop. Addressed indefinite challenge periods from defective notifications. Extended to financial decisions (CE, 2017) and implicit decisions. Now governs most situations where R421-5 would otherwise leave claims open indefinitely.

### Commune de Grande-Synthe — Conseil d'État, 19 novembre 2020, n° 427301
- **Facts:** The commune, vulnerable to climate change, challenged the insufficiency of State climate action to meet greenhouse gas reduction commitments under the Paris Agreement.
- **Rule:** A municipality directly affected by climate change has standing to challenge State climate action. The CE ordered the Government to justify its trajectory within three months.
- **Significance:** First use of injunction powers to compel climate compliance. The follow-up decision (CE, 1er juillet 2021) found the State had failed and issued further injunctions. Opened climate litigation in French administrative law.

### La Cimade et GISTI — Conseil d'État, 7 février 2012, n° 350382 (circulaires)
- **Facts:** Associations challenged ministerial circulars on immigration enforcement, arguing they modified the applicable legal framework.
- **Rule:** A circular containing imperative provisions is a reviewable decision, even if formally addressed only to agents. If it adds to the legal order or misinterprets law, it may be annulled.
- **Significance:** Refined the Duvignères jurisprudence (CE Sect., 2002): circulars with normative content are subject to judicial review. Prevents circumventing scrutiny through binding instructions disguised as internal documents.

### Société Eden — Conseil d'État Sect., 6 décembre 2013, n° 365155
- **Facts:** A company challenged a decision denying it an authorization, arguing that the administration had failed to apply a principle of proportionality when weighing competing interests.
- **Rule:** The administrative judge must exercise a proportionality review (contrôle de proportionnalité) when the administration exercises discretionary power affecting fundamental freedoms or economic interests.
- **Significance:** Strengthened the intensity of judicial review over administrative discretion, especially in economic regulation. The CE moved from a limited review (erreur manifeste d'appréciation) to a full proportionality analysis in areas where fundamental rights are at stake.

## Réformes récentes (2023-2026)

### CRPA — Évolutions et codification continue

The CRPA, created by ordonnance n° 2015-1341 of 23 October 2015, continues to evolve:

- **Décret n° 2023-660 du 26 juillet 2023** : clarified the lists of procedures where silence means rejection (rather than acceptance under L231-1).
- **Dématérialisation renforcée** : mandatory online filing (téléprocédures) expanded pursuant to CRPA L112-9. Since 2024, most standard requests to State services must be submitted electronically.
- **Renforcement du droit à régularisation** (CRPA L114-6) : before rejecting a request on formal grounds, the administration must invite the applicant to regularize.

### Réforme des juridictions administratives

- **Loi n° 2023-1059 du 20 novembre 2023** (dispositions applicables aux juridictions administratives) : measures to accelerate procedures, including expanded single-judge formations (juge unique) and reinforced mediation.
- **Médiation administrative** (CJA L213-1 et suivants) : progressively reinforced since 2016, mediation is now routinely proposed at the initial stage of proceedings, particularly in public employment and tax disputes.
- **Contentieux sériels** : specialized procedures for repetitive claims (pensions, permits, social benefits). The CE encourages "pilote" judgments for recurring legal questions.
- **Aide juridictionnelle** : income thresholds updated by decrees in 2024-2025 to improve access to administrative justice.

### Czabaj et suites — Consolidation du délai raisonnable

Since the Czabaj decision of 2016, the "reasonable time" principle has been progressively extended and refined:

- **Extension aux décisions implicites** : CE, 18 mars 2019, n° 417270 — the one-year limit applies to implicit decisions (silence vaut rejet), even absent formal notification.
- **Extension aux décisions financières** : CE, 31 mars 2017, n° 389842 — applied to financial entitlements (pensions, salaries, benefits).
- **Circonstances exceptionnelles** : case law is restrictive. Recognized: hospitalization, decision addressed to wrong person. Rejected: ignorance of rights, administrative complexity.
- **Interaction avec le CRPA** : even when the administration fails to acknowledge receipt (R112-5), the one-year backstop applies from the date the applicant reasonably knew of the decision.
- **Proposition de codification** : the Conseil d'État rapport public 2024 recommended codifying Czabaj in the CJA. As of early 2026, no legislative text adopted, but the principle is firmly established.

### Autres évolutions notables

- **Contentieux climatique et environnemental** : following Grande-Synthe (2020-2021), administrative courts continue to examine climate claims. The scope of justiciable climate obligations and injunction powers (L911-1) remains a developing area.
- **Open data** : implementation of CRPA L300-2 et suivants has expanded obligations on administrations to publish data proactively. Disputes over refusal to communicate data have increased.
- **Évolution du référé-liberté** : the scope of "libertés fondamentales" under L521-2 continues to expand, with the CE recognizing new freedoms eligible for emergency protection (droit d'asile, droit au logement opposable).

## Common Questions & Patterns

- "Can I challenge an administrative decision?" → Start with CJA R421-1: was the decision notified with time-limit information (R421-5)? If not, the 2-month deadline may not have started running — but note the Czabaj one-year backstop. Identify the type of decision (individual vs. regulatory) and whether prior administrative appeal (recours administratif préalable obligatoire — RAPO) is required. Then file a recours pour excès de pouvoir before the competent tribunal administratif.

- "How do I get an urgent injunction against the administration?" → Use CJA L521-1 (référé-suspension): must demonstrate urgency and a serious doubt as to legality. Or use CJA L521-2 (référé-liberté) if a fundamental freedom is seriously and manifestly threatened — the judge has 48 hours to rule. Or CJA L521-3 (référé mesures utiles) for other urgent conservatory measures.

- "The administration has not responded to my request — what now?" → Apply CRPA L231-1: after 2 months of silence, acceptance is the default rule, subject to a list of exceptions set by decree (L231-4). If silence means rejection (exceptions), the 2-month appeal deadline runs from the expiry of the 2-month response period. Note: Czabaj one-year backstop applies to implicit decisions as well.

- "Can I access an administrative document?" → CRPA L311-1 grants a general right of access. The CADA (Commission d'accès aux documents administratifs) must be consulted before any judicial challenge. Documents relating to national security, individual privacy, or ongoing proceedings may be excluded (L311-5 and L311-6).

- "The administration caused me damage — can I sue?" → Administrative courts have jurisdiction (Blanco). Establish whether there is a faute de service (organizational fault) or personal fault by an agent. Strict liability (sans faute) applies in certain cases (risk, equality before public burdens). Time limit: 4 years from the end of the year in which the claim arose (loi du 31 décembre 1968).

- "Can a third party challenge a public contract?" → Yes, under the Tarn-et-Garonne jurisprudence (CE Ass., 2014). A third party with sufficiently direct and certain interests may bring a full-jurisdiction action. The judge weighs the illegality against the public interest in contract stability.

- "Are administrative guidelines (circulaires, notes de service) binding?" → If they contain imperative provisions or produce legal effects, they are reviewable acts (Duvignères, 2002; GISTI, 2020 on lignes directrices). They can be challenged by REP and annulled if they add to or misinterpret applicable law.
