# Labor Law — French Labor Law

## Applicable Codes
- Code du travail (C. trav.) — consolidated, Parts L (legislative), R (regulatory), D (decree)
  - Employment contract: L1221-1 et seq.
  - Termination: L1231-1 et seq. (CDI), L1242-1 et seq. (CDD)
  - Working time: L3121-1 et seq.
  - Collective relations / trade unions: L2141-1 et seq.
  - Health and safety: L4121-1 et seq.
- Specific laws: loi n° 2016-1088 of 8 Aug. 2016 (*loi El Khomri* — travail); ordonnances Macron of 22 Sept. 2017 (n° 2017-1387 et al.); loi n° 2018-771 of 5 Sept. 2018 (*avenir professionnel*)

## Key Articles

### Formation du contrat de travail

#### L1221-1 — Employment Contract (Contrat de Travail)
> « Le contrat de travail est soumis aux règles du droit commun. Il peut être établi selon les formes que les contractants décident d'adopter. »
> *Judicial definition (Cass. soc.)*: A contract of employment requires three elements — a *prestation de travail*, *rémunération*, and *lien de subordination juridique* (legal subordination to the employer's direction, control, and disciplinary power).
Keywords: contrat de travail, subordination, requalification, droit commun
Source: Legifrance / Cass. soc., 13 nov. 1996 (Société Générale)

#### L1221-2 — Written Form and Language
> The CDI need not be in writing (except where a collective agreement requires it); all contracts must be drafted in French. A foreign-language clause is unenforceable against the employee.
Keywords: écrit, langue française, CDI, forme
Source: Legifrance

#### L1221-6 — Pre-employment Inquiries
> Information requested from a job candidate must have a direct and necessary link with the proposed position or with the evaluation of the candidate's professional aptitude. The candidate must be informed beforehand of the methods and techniques used.
Keywords: recrutement, vie privée, pertinence, information candidat
Source: Legifrance

#### L1221-10 — Déclaration Préalable à l'Embauche (DPAE)
> The employer must make a prior declaration to the URSSAF before any hiring. Failure constitutes an offence of *travail dissimulé*.
Keywords: DPAE, URSSAF, embauche, travail dissimulé
Source: Legifrance

### Période d'essai

#### L1221-19 — Purpose of the Trial Period
> « La période d'essai permet à l'employeur d'évaluer les compétences du salarié dans son travail, notamment au regard de son expérience, et au salarié d'apprécier si les fonctions occupées lui conviennent. »
Keywords: période d'essai, évaluation, compétences, objet
Source: Legifrance

#### L1221-20 — Express Stipulation Required
> The trial period and any renewal must be expressly stipulated in the employment contract or the letter of engagement.
Keywords: période d'essai, clause expresse, lettre d'engagement
Source: Legifrance

#### L1221-21 — Maximum Duration (CDI)
> Maximum initial durations: *ouvriers/employés* — 2 months; *agents de maîtrise/techniciens* — 3 months; *cadres* — 4 months. Renewable once if provided by collective agreement and the contract.
Keywords: durée maximale, catégorie professionnelle, renouvellement
Source: Legifrance

#### L1221-25 — Notice of Termination During Trial
> During the trial period, termination requires prior notice: 24 hours if less than 8 days' presence; 48 hours between 8 days and 1 month; 2 weeks after 1 month; 1 month after 3 months (employer-initiated). Employee: 48 hours (24 hours if less than 8 days).
Keywords: délai de prévenance, rupture période d'essai
Source: Legifrance

### Contrat à durée déterminée (CDD)

#### L1242-1 — Prohibition of Permanent Use
> « Un contrat de travail à durée déterminée, quel que soit son motif, ne peut avoir ni pour objet ni pour effet de pourvoir durablement un emploi lié à l'activité normale et permanente de l'entreprise. »
Keywords: CDD, emploi permanent, activité normale, requalification
Source: Legifrance

#### L1242-2 — Permitted Grounds for CDD
> A CDD may only be concluded in specified cases: replacement of an absent employee, temporary increase in activity, seasonal employment, specific *contrats d'usage* in designated sectors, replacement before post suppression, etc.
Keywords: cas de recours, remplacement, accroissement temporaire, saisonnier, usage
Source: Legifrance

#### L1242-8 — Maximum Duration
> The CDD (including renewals) may not exceed 18 months in most cases; 9 months when awaiting a permanent hire; 24 months for contracts performed abroad or for specific orders/exports.
Keywords: durée maximale CDD, renouvellement, 18 mois
Source: Legifrance

#### L1243-1 — Early Termination of CDD
> « Sauf accord des parties, le contrat de travail à durée déterminée ne peut être rompu avant l'échéance du terme qu'en cas de faute grave, de force majeure ou d'inaptitude constatée par le médecin du travail. »
Keywords: rupture anticipée, faute grave, force majeure, inaptitude
Source: Legifrance

#### L1243-8 — End-of-Contract Indemnity (Indemnité de Précarité)
> At the end of the CDD, the employee receives an indemnity of at least 10% of total gross remuneration (6% if a collective agreement provides counterpart training access).
Keywords: indemnité de précarité, 10%, fin de CDD
Source: Legifrance

### Licenciement pour motif personnel

#### L1232-1 — Requirement of Real and Serious Cause
> « Tout licenciement pour motif personnel est motivé dans les conditions définies par le présent chapitre. Il est justifié par une cause réelle et sérieuse. »
Keywords: cause réelle et sérieuse, motif personnel, motivation
Source: Legifrance

#### L1232-2 — Prior Interview (Entretien Préalable)
> The employer must summon the employee to a prior interview by registered letter or hand-delivered letter, specifying the subject, date, time, and place. Minimum 5 working days' notice.
Keywords: entretien préalable, convocation, lettre recommandée, délai
Source: Legifrance

#### L1232-4 — Right to Assistance at Interview
> The employee may be accompanied by a person of their choice belonging to the company's staff or, if no staff representatives exist, by an external *conseiller du salarié* listed by the préfet.
Keywords: assistance, conseiller du salarié, entretien préalable
Source: Legifrance

#### L1232-6 — Notification of Dismissal
> Dismissal is notified by registered letter with acknowledgement of receipt, setting out the precise reasons. The letter fixes the scope of the dispute (*les limites du litige*).
Keywords: lettre de licenciement, motivation, LRAR
Source: Legifrance

#### L1234-1 — Notice Period (Préavis)
> « Lorsque le contrat de travail est rompu par l'employeur, le salarié a droit : s'il justifie chez le même employeur d'une ancienneté de services continus inférieure à six mois, à un préavis dont la durée est déterminée par la loi, la convention collective ou, à défaut, par les usages pratiqués dans la localité et la profession. Pour une ancienneté entre six mois et deux ans : un mois. Pour une ancienneté d'au moins deux ans : deux mois. »
Keywords: préavis, ancienneté, durée, dispense
Source: Legifrance

#### L1234-9 — Statutory Severance Pay (Indemnité Légale de Licenciement)
> « Le salarié titulaire d'un contrat de travail à durée indéterminée, licencié alors qu'il compte huit mois d'ancienneté ininterrompus au service du même employeur, a droit, sauf en cas de faute grave, à une indemnité de licenciement. »
> *Amount*: At least 1/4 of a month's salary per year for the first 10 years, then 1/3 per year thereafter (R1234-2).
Keywords: indemnité légale, ancienneté, 8 mois, faute grave exclusion
Source: Legifrance

#### L1235-3 — Barème Macron (Scale for Unfair Dismissal Damages)
> « Si le licenciement d'un salarié survient pour une cause qui n'est pas réelle et sérieuse, le juge peut proposer la réintégration du salarié dans l'entreprise. Lorsque la réintégration est refusée par l'une ou l'autre des parties, le juge octroie au salarié une indemnité à la charge de l'employeur, dont le montant est compris entre les montants minimaux et maximaux fixés dans le tableau ci-après. »
> *Scale (selected)*: 0 years seniority: min 0, max 1 month; 1 year: min 1, max 2 months; 2 years: min 3, max 3.5 months; 10 years: min 3, max 10 months; 30+ years: min 3, max 20 months.
Keywords: barème Macron, indemnité, plafond, plancher, ancienneté
Source: Legifrance (Art. L1235-3 as amended by ord. n° 2017-1387)

#### L1235-3-1 — Exclusions from the Barème (Nullity Cases)
> The barème does not apply when the dismissal is null — i.e., related to discrimination, harassment, violation of a fundamental freedom, whistleblower protection, maternity/paternity. In such cases, minimum damages are 6 months' salary with no cap.
Keywords: nullité, discrimination, harcèlement, liberté fondamentale, plancher 6 mois
Source: Legifrance

### Licenciement économique

#### L1233-1 — Definition of Economic Ground
> Constitutes an economic dismissal any dismissal effected by an employer for one or more reasons not inherent in the person of the employee, resulting from a suppression or transformation of the position, or a modification of an essential element of the contract refused by the employee, consequent upon economic difficulties, technological changes, cessation of activity, or reorganization necessary to safeguard competitiveness.
Keywords: motif économique, suppression de poste, difficultés économiques, réorganisation, sauvegarde compétitivité
Source: Legifrance

#### L1233-3 — Quantified Criteria for Economic Difficulties
> Economic difficulties characterized by decline in orders/revenue over consecutive quarters (1 quarter <11 employees, 2 for 11-49, 3 for 50-299, 4 for 300+), operating losses, cash-flow degradation, or other justifying elements.
Keywords: indicateurs économiques, baisse chiffre d'affaires, trimestres, seuils
Source: Legifrance (as amended by ord. n° 2017-1387)

#### L1233-4 — Reclassification Obligation
> Employer must make all reclassification efforts within the company/group on equivalent or (with consent) lower positions. Offer must be precise and written.
Keywords: obligation de reclassement, postes disponibles, groupe, offre écrite
Source: Legifrance

#### L1233-61 — Plan de Sauvegarde de l'Emploi (PSE)
> Required in companies 50+ employees dismissing 10+ over 30 days. Includes measures to avoid/limit dismissals and facilitate reclassification. Submitted to DREETS for validation (negotiated) or homologation (unilateral).
Keywords: PSE, plan social, 50 salariés, DREETS, validation, homologation
Source: Legifrance

### Rupture conventionnelle

#### L1237-11 — Agreed Termination (Rupture Conventionnelle)
> « L'employeur et le salarié peuvent convenir en commun des conditions de la rupture du contrat de travail qui les lie. La rupture conventionnelle, exclusive du licenciement ou de la démission, ne peut être imposée par l'une ou l'autre des parties. »
Keywords: rupture conventionnelle, consentement mutuel, CDI
Source: Legifrance

#### L1237-12 — Meetings and Assistance
> One or more meetings between the parties. The employee may be assisted by a person of their choice (staff member or external *conseiller*); if the employee is assisted, the employer may also be assisted.
Keywords: entretiens, assistance, conseiller du salarié
Source: Legifrance

#### L1237-13 — Withdrawal Period and Homologation
> Each party has 15 calendar days from signing to withdraw. After the withdrawal period expires, the request is sent to the DREETS, which has 15 working days to approve (*homologation*). Silence = approval.
Keywords: délai de rétractation, 15 jours, homologation, DREETS
Source: Legifrance

### Harcèlement moral et sexuel

#### L1152-1 — Moral Harassment (Harcèlement Moral)
> « Aucun salarié ne doit subir les agissements répétés de harcèlement moral qui ont pour objet ou pour effet une dégradation de ses conditions de travail susceptible de porter atteinte à ses droits et à sa dignité, d'altérer sa santé physique ou mentale ou de compromettre son avenir professionnel. »
Keywords: harcèlement moral, agissements répétés, dégradation, dignité, santé
Source: Legifrance

#### L1152-2 — Protection Against Retaliation
> No employee may be sanctioned, dismissed, or subjected to discriminatory treatment for having suffered, refused to suffer, or testified about moral harassment.
Keywords: protection, représailles, témoin, sanction
Source: Legifrance

#### L1152-4 — Employer's Prevention Duty
> The employer must take all necessary measures to prevent moral harassment.
Keywords: prévention, obligation employeur, mesures
Source: Legifrance

#### L1153-1 — Sexual Harassment
> No employee shall be subjected to acts of sexual harassment, defined as: (1) repeated remarks or behavior with a sexual or sexist connotation that undermine dignity or create an intimidating, hostile, or offensive environment; or (2) any form of serious pressure, even non-repeated, for the real or apparent purpose of obtaining a sexual act.
Keywords: harcèlement sexuel, connotation sexuelle, pression grave, environnement hostile
Source: Legifrance (as amended by loi n° 2021-1018 of 2 Aug. 2021)

#### L1153-5 — Employer's Obligation Regarding Sexual Harassment
> The employer must take all necessary measures to prevent sexual harassment and must display in the workplace the text of the applicable Penal Code provisions (Art. 222-33 CP) and the available remedies.
Keywords: prévention, affichage, information, sanctions pénales
Source: Legifrance

### Discrimination

#### L1132-1 — General Prohibition of Discrimination
> No person may be excluded from recruitment, sanctioned, dismissed, or subjected to discriminatory treatment on the basis of 25+ protected criteria including: origin, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, age, family situation, pregnancy, genetic characteristics, economic vulnerability, ethnicity, political opinions, trade union activity, right to strike, religious beliefs, physical appearance, surname, place of residence, health, disability, or loss of autonomy.
Keywords: discrimination, critères prohibés, recrutement, sanction, 25 critères
Source: Legifrance

#### L1134-1 — Burden of Proof in Discrimination Cases
> The employee presents factual elements suggesting the existence of discrimination. The burden then shifts to the employer to prove that the decision was justified by objective elements unrelated to any discrimination.
Keywords: charge de la preuve, aménagement, éléments de fait, justification objective
Source: Legifrance

### Temps de travail

#### L3121-1 — Definition of Effective Working Time
> « La durée du travail effectif est le temps pendant lequel le salarié est à la disposition de l'employeur et se conforme à ses directives sans pouvoir vaquer librement à des occupations personnelles. »
Keywords: temps de travail effectif, disposition, directives
Source: Legifrance

#### L3121-18 — Maximum Daily Duration
> The daily working time may not exceed 10 hours of effective work. Derogations possible by collective agreement or administrative authorization (up to 12 hours).
Keywords: durée maximale quotidienne, 10 heures, dérogation
Source: Legifrance

#### L3121-20 — Maximum Weekly Duration (Absolute)
> In no event may the weekly working time exceed 48 hours in a single week.
Keywords: durée maximale hebdomadaire, 48 heures, absolue
Source: Legifrance

#### L3121-22 — Maximum Weekly Duration (Average)
> The average weekly working time, calculated over any period of 12 consecutive weeks, may not exceed 44 hours (may be raised to 46 by collective agreement).
Keywords: durée moyenne, 44 heures, 12 semaines, dérogation conventionnelle
Source: Legifrance

#### L3121-27 — Legal Working Time (Durée Légale — 35 Heures)
> « La durée légale de travail effectif des salariés à temps complet est fixée à trente-cinq heures par semaine. »
Keywords: durée légale, 35 heures, temps complet, seuil déclenchement heures supplémentaires
Source: Legifrance

#### L3121-28 — Overtime (Heures Supplémentaires)
> Any hour worked beyond 35 hours per week constitutes overtime and gives rise to increased pay or equivalent rest.
Keywords: heures supplémentaires, majoration, repos compensateur, 35 heures
Source: Legifrance

#### L3121-64 — Forfait Jours (Lump-Sum Day Agreement)
> Autonomous cadres or employees whose functions do not permit following collective hours may conclude a *forfait jours* agreement: up to 218 days per year. Requires a collective agreement and individual written consent.
Keywords: forfait jours, 218 jours, cadres autonomes, accord collectif, autonomie
Source: Legifrance

### Congés payés

#### L3141-1 — Right to Paid Leave
> Every employee is entitled to paid annual leave at the employer's expense.
Keywords: congés payés, droit, charge employeur
Source: Legifrance

#### L3141-3 — Accrual of Leave
> The employee accrues 2.5 working days of leave per month of effective work, up to a maximum of 30 working days (5 weeks) per year.
Keywords: acquisition, 2,5 jours, mois de travail effectif, 30 jours

#### L3141-5 — Periods Assimilated to Effective Work
> Certain absences are treated as effective work for leave accrual purposes: paid leave itself, maternity/paternity/adoption leave, occupational accidents (up to one uninterrupted year), and training time.
Keywords: périodes assimilées, maternité, accident du travail, formation
Source: Legifrance

#### L3141-16 — Employer Sets the Leave Period and Order
> The employer determines the leave period and the order of departures, after consulting the CSE, taking into account family situation, seniority, and multiple-employer constraints.
Keywords: période de congés, ordre des départs, CSE, critères
Source: Legifrance

### Négociation collective

#### L2231-1 — Capacity to Negotiate Collective Agreements
> Collective agreements are negotiated between employers (or employer organizations) and representative trade unions.
Keywords: convention collective, accord collectif, syndicats représentatifs, négociation
Source: Legifrance

#### L2232-12 — Validity of Enterprise-Level Agreements
> An enterprise-level agreement is valid if signed by one or more representative trade unions having obtained, together, more than 50% of votes cast in the first round of CSE elections; or, failing that, if signed by unions representing more than 30% and approved by a majority referendum of employees.

Source: Legifrance (as amended by ord. n° 2017-1385)

### Santé et sécurité au travail

#### L4121-1 — Employer's Safety Obligation (Obligation de Sécurité)
> « L'employeur prend les mesures nécessaires pour assurer la sécurité et protéger la santé physique et mentale des travailleurs. Ces mesures comprennent : 1° Des actions de prévention des risques professionnels et de la pénibilité au travail ; 2° Des actions d'information et de formation ; 3° La mise en place d'une organisation et de moyens adaptés. »
Keywords: obligation de sécurité, prévention, santé physique et mentale, moyens adaptés
Source: Legifrance

#### L4121-2 — General Prevention Principles
> The employer implements prevention measures on the basis of nine general principles: avoid risks, evaluate unavoidable risks, combat risks at source, adapt work to the person, take account of the state of technology, replace the dangerous with the non-dangerous or less dangerous, plan prevention, give priority to collective protection, and give appropriate instructions.
Keywords: principes généraux de prévention, 9 principes, évaluation, substitution
Source: Legifrance

#### L4121-3 — Risk Assessment (Document Unique — DUERP)
> The employer must assess occupational risks, including in the choice of manufacturing processes, equipment, chemical substances, and workspace layout. The results are recorded in the *document unique d'évaluation des risques professionnels* (DUERP).
Keywords: DUERP, évaluation des risques, document unique, mise à jour
Source: Legifrance

#### L4131-1 — Employee's Right of Withdrawal (*Droit de Retrait*)
> An employee who has reasonable grounds to believe that a work situation presents a serious and imminent danger to their life or health may withdraw from that situation. The employer may not impose any sanction or wage deduction for exercising this right in good faith.
Keywords: droit de retrait, danger grave et imminent, bonne foi, sanction interdite
Source: Legifrance

### Droit de grève

#### L2511-1 — Right to Strike
> « L'exercice du droit de grève ne peut justifier la rupture du contrat de travail, sauf faute lourde imputable au salarié. Tout licenciement prononcé en absence de faute lourde est nul de plein droit. »
Keywords: grève, protection, nullité, faute lourde
Source: Legifrance

## Core Principles

- **Lien de subordination (Subordination test)**: The existence of an employment contract is determined by the factual presence of subordination — the employer's power to give instructions, control execution, and discipline the worker. This test is assessed regardless of how the parties label their relationship (*requalification* is possible). Cass. soc., 13 nov. 1996.
- **Cause réelle et sérieuse**: Any dismissal for personal reasons must rest on an objective, existing, and sufficiently important cause. The employer bears the burden of proof. Art. L1232-1. Courts undertake a full review of the facts.
- **Faveur (In dubio pro labore)**: Where a conflict exists between a statute, collective agreement, or individual contract, the more favorable provision for the worker applies, unless the law expressly provides otherwise.
- **Obligation de sécurité de résultat (pre-2015) / moyens renforcés (post-2015)**: The employer has a duty to protect workers' physical and mental health. After Cass. soc., 25 nov. 2015 (*Air France*), the obligation evolved from strict liability (*résultat*) to a reinforced obligation of means — the employer exonerates itself by proving it took all necessary measures under Art. L4121-1.
- **Égalité de traitement**: Equal pay for equal work; prohibition of discrimination on enumerated grounds (Art. L1132-1 — 25 prohibited criteria including origin, sex, age, disability, union activity).

## Landmark Decisions

### Brinon — Cass. soc., 31 mai 1956
- **Facts**: Following a reorganization, an employer dismissed several employees whose posts were eliminated for economic reasons, without a specific statutory framework for economic dismissals existing at the time.
- **Rule**: An employer may lawfully dismiss employees for economic reasons (suppression of posts), provided the decision is genuine and not a pretext; this established the category of *licenciement économique* as a distinct ground of dismissal.
- **Significance**: The founding decision for economic dismissal doctrine in French law; the statutory framework was later built around this principle (now Arts. L1233-1 et seq. C. trav.).

### Société Générale — Cass. soc., 13 nov. 1996, n° 94-13.187
- **Facts**: A financial trader worked under a contract labelled as a service/commercial agreement with a financial institution; tax authorities and the worker argued it was in fact an employment contract.
- **Rule**: The existence of an employment contract does not depend on the label the parties give their relationship; it is determined by the factual conditions of performance — specifically, whether a *lien de subordination juridique* exists.
- **Significance**: Definitive statement of the subordination test as the sole criterion for employment contract qualification; enables courts to *requalify* commercial, freelance, or platform-worker contracts into employment contracts.

### Nikon — Cass. soc., 2 oct. 2001, n° 99-42.942
- **Facts**: An employer accessed and used emails stored on an employee's work computer to justify a disciplinary dismissal; the emails were in a folder labelled "personal."
- **Rule**: An employee has a right to privacy and confidentiality of correspondence even within the workplace; an employer may not open emails identified as personal by the employee without the employee being present or duly notified.
- **Significance**: Foundational decision on employee privacy at work; established that Art. 9 C. civ. applies in employment relationships; triggered widespread adoption of IT-use policies (*chartes informatiques*) in French companies.

### Barbier — Cass. soc., 12 juill. 2004 (Barbier c/ Époux Imbert)
- **Facts**: An employer classified a worker as a self-employed contractor (*travailleur indépendant*) despite conditions of work that showed classic subordination (fixed hours, employer-provided tools, integration into organized service).
- **Rule**: Regardless of contractual labels, if the hallmarks of subordination are present — direction, control, disciplinary power — the relationship must be requalified as a *contrat de travail*. The principle of reality (*réalité*) prevails over the formal appearance.
- **Significance**: Reinforced the *Société Générale* (1996) requalification doctrine; frequently cited in platform-worker and gig-economy disputes to requalify self-employed status into employment.

### Air France — Cass. soc., 25 nov. 2015, n° 14-24.444
- **Facts**: An employee developed occupational disease (asbestosis); the employer argued it had taken all available precautions given the state of knowledge at the time.
- **Rule**: The employer's safety obligation is no longer purely one of *résultat* (strict liability regardless of precautions taken); the employer can exonerate itself by proving it took all necessary measures prescribed by Arts. L4121-1 and L4121-2 C. trav.
- **Significance**: Overturned the prior strict-liability approach to occupational health; rebalanced employer liability while maintaining a high standard of preventive action.

### Baby Loup — Cass. ass. plén., 25 juin 2014, n° 13-28.369
- **Facts**: Childcare assistant dismissed from a private nursery for wearing a religious veil in violation of the *règlement intérieur* imposing neutrality.
- **Rule**: A private employer may restrict religious expression through internal rules if justified by the nature of tasks and proportionate to the aim. Must be precise, not a blanket ban.
- **Significance**: Major ruling on *laïcité* in the private sector; proportionality analysis required case-by-case. Influenced Art. L1321-2-1 C. trav. (loi Travail 2016).

### Uber — Cass. soc., 4 mars 2020, n° 19-13.316
- **Facts**: VTC driver challenged independent-contractor status; Uber set pricing, imposed routes, applied ratings-based sanctions.
- **Rule**: When a platform sets pricing, controls service conditions, and exercises sanction power, subordination is established — requalification as employment contract.
- **Significance**: Landmark gig-economy ruling applying the subordination test to platform workers; influenced EU platform-worker directive discussions.

### Cass. soc., 29 juin 2011, n° 09-71.107 (Harcèlement moral — méthode de gestion)
- **Facts**: Employee alleged systematic management practices (excessive pressure, humiliation, unreasonable objectives) constituted harassment, though applied to the entire team.
- **Rule**: Management methods imposing constant pressure or demeaning treatment may constitute moral harassment (Art. L1152-1) even when collective and non-targeted.
- **Significance**: Extended moral harassment to systemic managerial practices (*harcèlement managérial*); employers must review management styles under their prevention obligation.

### Cass. soc., 3 nov. 2011, n° 10-18.036 (Discrimination syndicale — comparaison de carrière)
- **Facts**: Union delegate claimed career discrimination — salary and promotions significantly behind comparable non-union colleagues.
- **Rule**: Courts compare career trajectory of the union representative with a panel of comparable colleagues; significant unjustified gap creates a presumption of discrimination.
- **Significance**: Established the *panel comparison method* for proving union discrimination.

### Cass. soc., 29 janv. 2003, n° 00-44.781 (Clause de non-concurrence — contrepartie financière)
- **Facts**: Non-compete clause without any financial compensation for the post-termination restriction period.
- **Rule**: A non-compete clause is null unless it provides an adequate *contrepartie financière* paid after contract termination.
- **Significance**: Mandatory validity condition for all non-compete clauses; without financial counterpart, void and unenforceable.

### Chronopost Barème — Cass. soc., 11 mai 2022, n° 21-14.490 (Validation of Barème Macron)
- **Facts**: Employee challenged the constitutionality and ECHR-compatibility of the *barème Macron* (Art. L1235-3), arguing it prevented full reparation of harm.
- **Rule**: The *barème Macron* is compatible with Art. 10 of ILO Convention n° 158 and Art. 6 ECHR; national courts must apply the scale but may depart from it only in individual cases of manifest incompatibility — a position later narrowed to essentially no judicial override by the *Assemblée plénière* in 2022.
- **Significance**: Confirmed the binding nature of the barème on all French labor courts; ended years of divergent lower-court rulings that had departed from the scale.

### Cass. ass. plén., 17 nov. 2023, n° 21-20.776 (Congés payés et maladie)
- **Facts**: Employee on long-term sick leave claimed paid-leave accrual during illness, relying on EU Directive 2003/88/EC (Art. 7).
- **Rule**: Sick leave (occupational or not) must count for paid-leave accrual under EU law; French provisions limiting accrual during non-occupational sick leave were set aside.
- **Significance**: Led to loi n° 2024-364 of 22 April 2024 amending Art. L3141-5: 2 days/month accrual during non-occupational sick leave (cap 24 days/year); no one-year cap for occupational illness/accident.

### Cass. soc., 13 sept. 2023, n° 22-17.340 (Forfait jours — suivi effectif)
- **Facts**: Employer applied *forfait jours* without ensuring regular workload monitoring as required by the collective agreement.
- **Rule**: *Forfait jours* is null if the collective agreement lacks sufficient monitoring guarantees or if the employer fails to implement them. Employee then entitled to overtime pay beyond 35 hours.
- **Significance**: Strict judicial control over *forfait jours*; employers must demonstrate active workload-monitoring procedures.

## Réformes récentes (2023-2026)

### Ordonnances Macron (2017) — Long-Term Impact
- **Ord. n° 2017-1387**: Barème Macron (Art. L1235-3) capping unfair-dismissal damages. Rupture conventionnelle collective (Arts. L1237-19+). Quantified economic-dismissal criteria (Art. L1233-3). Merged DP/CE/CHSCT into the CSE.
- **Ord. n° 2017-1385**: Enterprise agreements prevail over branch in *bloc 3* (Art. L2253-3); 13 branch-reserved topics (*bloc 1*); branch-lockable topics (*bloc 2*).
- **Status (2026)**: Barème binding (Cass. ass. plén. 2022). CSE universal. Enterprise-agreement primacy standard.

### Loi Avenir Professionnel (2018)
- **Loi n° 2018-771 of 5 Sept. 2018**: Created *France Compétences*. Replaced CIF with CPF de transition. Monetized CPF (€500/year, €800 low-qualification). Liberalized apprenticeship (age limit 29, year-round entry, OPCA → OPCO). *Référent harcèlement* mandatory in CSE and companies 250+ employees.

### Réforme de l'assurance chômage (2023-2024)
- **Décret n° 2023-33 of 26 Jan. 2023**: Tightened eligibility — 6-month affiliation over 24 months. Contracyclical modulation: benefit duration ×0.75 when unemployment <9% (e.g., 24 months → 18 months).
- **Décret n° 2024-963**: Extended contracyclical mechanism; adjusted senior (55+) rules after social-partner negotiations. Progressive alignment planned through 2026.
- **Loi n° 2022-1598 of 21 Dec. 2022**: Authorized government decree-setting of unemployment rules absent social-partner agreement — greater state control over the *régime d'assurance chômage*.

### Partage de la valeur (2023)
- **Loi n° 2023-1107 of 29 Nov. 2023**: Transposed ANI of 10 Feb. 2023. (1) Companies 11-49 employees must implement at least one value-sharing device (*intéressement*, *participation*, PPV, or savings plan) if net profit ≥1% of revenue for 3 consecutive years (effective fiscal years from 1 Jan. 2025). (2) Enhanced PPV: up to €3,000/year (€6,000 with intéressement), social-contribution exemption for <50 employees / salaries <3 SMIC. (3) Mandatory branch negotiations on *participation* for sub-threshold companies.

### Congés payés — Mise en Conformité EU (2024)
- **Loi n° 2024-364 of 22 April 2024**: Following Cass. ass. plén. 17 Nov. 2023 and CJEU Directive 2003/88/EC case law. Non-occupational sick leave: 2 days/month accrual (cap 24 days/year). Occupational sick leave/accident: full 2.5 days/month, no one-year cap. Retroactive claims permitted. 15-month carry-over after return to work.

## Common Questions & Patterns

- **"What are my rights if dismissed (CDI)?"** → Employer must have a *cause réelle et sérieuse* (Art. L1232-1). Procedure: written convocation to a prior interview (*entretien préalable*, Art. L1232-2), interview with possibility to be assisted, written notification of dismissal (Art. L1232-6). Employee entitled to: notice period (Art. L1234-1) unless *faute grave* or *faute lourde*; statutory severance pay after 8 months' seniority (Art. L1234-9); *barème Macron* damages if no real and serious cause (Art. L1235-3).

- **"How does rupture conventionnelle work?"** → Arts. L1237-11 et seq.: mutual agreement, no imposition by either party. Procedure: one or more meetings, signing of a convention, 15-day withdrawal period (*délai de rétractation*), then approval (*homologation*) by the *DREETS* (formerly DIRECCTE) within 15 working days. Employee receives specific indemnity (at least equal to the *indemnité légale de licenciement*) and can claim unemployment benefits (*ARE*).

- **"What is the barème Macron and does it apply to my case?"** → Art. L1235-3: binding scale capping damages for unfair dismissal (no real and serious cause), expressed in months of gross salary, based solely on seniority. Applies to all CDI dismissals. Does NOT apply to: dismissals in violation of a fundamental freedom (Art. L1235-3-1), null dismissals (discrimination, harassment, maternity/paternity), or economic dismissal irregularities with specific damage provisions.

- **"Can my employer access my work emails?"** → *Nikon* doctrine: emails marked "personal" are covered by privacy protection even on work equipment. Employer may consult non-personal professional emails only; must notify employees of any monitoring (*charte informatique*); CNIL regulations apply. Covert monitoring without prior notification is generally inadmissible as evidence (Art. L1222-4 C. trav.).

- **"What is moral harassment and what can I do?"** → Art. L1152-1: repeated actions that degrade working conditions and affect the employee's rights, dignity, health, or professional future. No need to prove intent — effects suffice. Employer has an obligation to prevent and stop harassment (Art. L1152-4). Employee may: alert the *CSE*, file a complaint with the labor inspectorate, or bring a claim before the *conseil de prud'hommes*; criminal prosecution under Art. 222-33-2 CP (2 years + €30,000 fine).
