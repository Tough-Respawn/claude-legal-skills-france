# Legal Glossary — French / English

Alphabetical by French term. Each entry gives: the French term in bold, the English translation in italics, and a concise definition.

This glossary covers general legal terms, civil law, criminal law, procedure, business law, labor law, and digital/data protection law. French terms are treated as proper nouns and kept in their original form.

---

## A

**Acte authentique** / *Notarized deed* — A legal document drawn up and authenticated by a notaire (notary), officier d'état civil, or other public officer. Has full probative value and is directly enforceable (force exécutoire) without a court judgment.

**Acte de commerce** / *Commercial act* — An act listed under art. L. 110-1 Code de commerce (e.g., purchase for resale, banking, insurance) that subjects the person performing it habitually to commercial law and to commercial courts (tribunal de commerce).

**Acte sous seing privé** / *Private deed* — A legal document signed by the parties without the intervention of a public officer. Admissible in evidence but does not carry the enforceable force of an acte authentique.

**Action en justice** / *Legal action / Lawsuit* — The right to bring a claim before a court and obtain a ruling on the merits. Requires standing (qualité), a legal interest (intérêt), and admissibility conditions.

**Appel** / *Appeal* — A second-degree review of a first-instance judgment by a cour d'appel. The appellate court re-examines facts and law (effet dévolutif). Time limit: generally 1 month from service of the judgment in civil matters (CPC art. 538).

**Arrêt** / *Judgment (collegial court)* — A decision rendered by a court sitting collegially: cour d'appel, Cour de cassation, Conseil d'État, or cour administrative d'appel. Distinguished from a jugement (first-instance) or ordonnance (single judge).

**Arrêt de règlement** / *Law-making judgment* — A judgment by which a court purports to lay down a general rule. Prohibited to ordinary courts since the post-Revolution period (art. 5 Code civil); however, the Cour de cassation effectively creates binding precedent through its jurisprudence constante.

**Assignation** / *Summons / Writ of claim* — The procedural document by which a claimant initiates civil proceedings, served on the defendant by a huissier (bailiff). States the claims, the grounds, and the court before which the defendant is summoned (CPC art. 54).

**Astreinte** / *Penalty per day of delay (astreinte)* — A financial penalty imposed by a court, accruing per day (or other unit of time) until the party complies with an order. Distinct from damages: purely coercive. Governed by loi n° 91-650 of 9 July 1991.

**Autorité de la chose jugée** / *Res judicata* — The binding effect of a final court judgment between the same parties on the same subject matter with the same cause of action. Precludes re-litigation (art. 1355 Code civil).

**Avocat** / *Lawyer (avocat)* — A licensed member of the bar (barreau) who provides legal advice and represents parties in court. Since 2012, avocat also encompasses the former avoué (appellate counsel). Different from avoué à la Cour (now abolished) and conseil juridique (now merged into avocat).

**Avocat général** / *Advocate General (at the Cour de cassation)* — A member of the parquet général at the Cour de cassation (or CJEU) who delivers a non-binding opinion (avis) to the court before deliberation, in the public interest.

---

## B

**Bloc de constitutionnalité** / *Constitutional bloc* — The ensemble of norms against which the Conseil constitutionnel measures the constitutionality of legislation. Includes: the Constitution of 4 October 1958, the Preamble of the Constitution of 1946, the Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen of 1789, the Charter of the Environment of 2004, and fundamental principles recognized by the laws of the Republic (PFRLRs). Established by Cons. const. 71-44 DC.

**Bonne foi** / *Good faith* — The principle requiring parties to perform their obligations loyally and honestly. Mandatory in contract performance (art. 1104 Code civil). Also governs pre-contractual negotiations (art. 1112) and interpretation (art. 1188).

---

## C

**Casier judiciaire** / *Criminal record* — The national register of criminal convictions. Has three bulletins: bulletin n° 1 (full record, for judicial and administrative use), bulletin n° 2 (partial, for employers in certain sectors), bulletin n° 3 (limited, given to the individual).

**Cause d'exonération** / *Ground of exoneration* — A circumstance that relieves a person of liability: force majeure, fault of the victim (faute de la victime), or the act of a third party (fait d'un tiers). Assessed differently in contractual and delictual liability.

**Chambre** / *Chamber (of a court)* — A specialized division of a court (e.g., chambre civile, chambre commerciale, chambre criminelle of the Cour de cassation; chambre sociale for labor matters).

**Clause abusive** / *Unfair term* — A contractual term that creates a significant imbalance between the rights and obligations of the parties to the detriment of the consumer, in a B2C contract. Prohibited under art. L. 131-1 Code de la consommation and EU Directive 93/13/EEC. Deemed unwritten (réputée non écrite).

**Clause limitative de responsabilité** / *Limitation of liability clause* — A contractual clause capping or excluding the debtor's liability for breach. Valid in principle in commercial contracts; may be invalidated if it contradicts the obligation essentielle (art. 1170 Code civil; Chronopost).

**Complicité** / *Complicity / Aiding and abetting* — Criminal participation by a person who aids, abets, or provokes an offence committed by the principal offender, without being the direct author. Governed by art. 121-7 Code pénal.

**Concurrence déloyale** / *Unfair competition* — Tortious acts by a competitor that breach trade usage, including denigrement (disparagement), confusion, imitation, and parasitism (parasitisme). Actionable under arts. 1240–1241 Code civil.

**Conseil constitutionnel** / *Constitutional Council* — The French quasi-judicial body that reviews the constitutionality of legislation. Exercises a priori review (before promulgation, art. 61) and a posteriori review via the QPC mechanism (art. 61-1). Not a court in the traditional sense but functions as France's constitutional court.

**Contrat de travail** / *Employment contract* — A contract by which a person (employee) performs work for another (employer) under a relationship of subordination (lien de subordination juridique), in exchange for remuneration. The subordination criterion is the key distinguishing feature from independent contractor relationships.

**Contravention** / *Petty offence (contravention)* — The least serious category of criminal offence in French law (below délit and crime). Punishable by fines only (up to €3,000 for 5th class). Tried by the tribunal de police.

**Convention collective** / *Collective bargaining agreement* — A written agreement between one or more employers (or employers' organizations) and one or more trade unions, governing wages, working conditions, and employment terms for the covered sector or company.

**Cour d'appel** / *Court of appeal* — The second-degree court that hears appeals from tribunaux judiciaires (civil/criminal) and specialized first-instance courts. France has 36 cours d'appel.

**Cour de cassation** / *Court of cassation (Supreme Court for private/criminal law)* — The highest court in the French judicial order for private and criminal matters. It does not review facts, only law. It can quash (casser) decisions and remand to a lower court, or rule definitively.

**Cour des comptes** / *Court of Audit* — The highest financial control body, responsible for auditing public accounts, certifying State accounts, and assessing the management of public institutions. Also has quasi-judicial functions.

---

## D

**Décret** / *Decree* — A regulatory act signed by the President of the Republic or the Prime Minister. Décrets en Conseil d'État require prior opinion of the Conseil d'État; décrets simples do not.

**Délit** / *Misdemeanor (délit)* — The middle category of criminal offence in France (between contravention and crime). Punishable by up to 10 years' imprisonment. Tried by the tribunal correctionnel.

**Dol** / *Fraud / Deceit (in contract law)* — A vice du consentement: intentional maneuver by one party to induce the other to contract (art. 1137 Code civil). Includes dolus by omission (réticence dolosive) where there is a duty to disclose.

**Dommages-intérêts** / *Damages* — Monetary compensation awarded to the victim of a contractual breach or a tort, intended to repair the prejudice suffered. The principle is full compensation (réparation intégrale) — no punitive damages in French law (except in limited statutory cases).

**Droit d'auteur** / *Copyright (droit d'auteur)* — The French system of copyright, which protects original works from the moment of creation without registration (CPI art. L. 111-1). Comprises moral rights (inalienable) and economic rights (transferable, lasting life + 70 years).

**Droit de la défense** / *Right of defense / Due process* — A constitutional principle guaranteeing every person the right to be heard, to know the charges against them, and to present a defense before any adverse decision.

**Droit de rétractation** / *Right of withdrawal* — The statutory right of a consumer to withdraw from a distance or off-premises contract within 14 days without giving reasons (art. L. 221-18 Code de la consommation; EU Directive 2011/83).

---

## E

**Effet direct** / *Direct effect* — The principle established in Van Gend en Loos (CJCE 1963) that EU law provisions that are clear, unconditional, and require no further action confer rights on individuals that national courts must enforce.

**Emprisonnement** / *Imprisonment* — A custodial sentence imposed for délits or crimes. For délits, maximum is 10 years; for crimes, from 15 years to life (réclusion criminelle à perpétuité).

**Erreur** / *Mistake (in contract law)* — A vice du consentement: a party's mistaken belief about an essential characteristic of the thing or person contracted with (art. 1132 Code civil). Excusable mistake about a quality that was determinative of consent can annul the contract.

**Exequatur** / *Exequatur* — The procedure by which a French court declares a foreign court judgment enforceable in France, allowing its coercive execution on French territory.

**Expertise judiciaire** / *Court-ordered expert report* — An investigation by a neutral expert (expert judiciaire) appointed by the court to provide technical findings on questions outside the court's competence. The expert's rapport (report) is evidence but does not bind the court.

---

## F

**Faute** / *Fault* — The basis of fault-based liability. In tort: any act falling below the standard of a reasonable person that causes damage to another (art. 1240 Code civil). In criminal law: intentional fault (faute intentionnelle), deliberate recklessness (mise en danger délibérée), or simple negligence (faute simple).

**Faute inexcusable** / *Inexcusable fault (in workplace accident law)* — An aggravated fault by the employer in occupational accident law: the employer had or should have had awareness of the particular risk and failed to take necessary measures. Triggers enhanced compensation (Code de la sécurité sociale art. L. 452-1).

**Fond** / *Merits* — The substantive examination of a case, as opposed to procedural admissibility (la forme). A decision au fond rules on the substance; a decision sur la forme rules only on procedure.

**Force majeure** / *Force majeure* — An event that is external, unforeseeable, and irresistible, which prevents performance of an obligation. Exonerates the debtor from contractual liability (art. 1218 Code civil) or the tortfeasor under some strict liability regimes.

**Frais irrépétibles** / *Non-recoverable costs (awarded under art. 700 CPC)* — Legal costs (mostly attorney fees) that a court may order the losing party to pay to the winning party under art. 700 Code de procédure civile, in equity.

---

## G

**Garde à vue** / *Police custody* — The temporary detention of a person by police for questioning, for a maximum of 24 hours (extendable to 48h in serious cases, longer for terrorism). The suspect has the right to notify a third party, to see a doctor, and to consult a lawyer from the first hour (CPP art. 63 et seq.).

**Gardien** / *Custodian (of a thing)* — In the law of strict liability for things (art. 1242 Code civil), the person who has the use, management, and control of a thing at the time it causes damage. Usually the owner, but can be a bailee.

**Garantie des vices cachés** / *Warranty against hidden defects* — A seller's legal warranty that the goods are free from hidden defects making them unfit for their intended use or substantially reducing their usefulness (Code civil art. 1641 et seq.). Action must be brought within 2 years of discovery of the defect.

---

## H

**Homologation** / *Judicial / administrative approval (homologation)* — The approval by a court or administrative authority of a private legal act, giving it enforceable force or confirming its legal validity. Example: homologation of a rupture conventionnelle by the DREETS (labor inspectorate).

**Huissier de justice** / *Bailiff (huissier de justice)* — A ministerial officer (officier ministériel) who serves legal documents, enforces court judgments and enforceable titles, and drafts official reports (constats). Since 2022, merged with commissaire-priseur to form the commissaire de justice.

---

## I

**Immunité** / *Immunity* — Protection from liability or jurisdiction. Examples: parliamentary immunity (immunité parlementaire), diplomatic immunity, and the Costedoat employee immunity in tort.

**In limine litis** / *Preliminary objection (in limine litis)* — A procedural challenge raised at the outset of proceedings, before any argument on the merits. Examples: objection to jurisdiction, objection to the admissibility of the claim, exception de nullité.

**Injonction** / *Injunction* — A court order requiring a party to perform or refrain from a specific act. In France, injunctions are mainly enforced via the astreinte mechanism.

**Instruction (judiciaire)** / *Judicial investigation* — A pre-trial investigative phase conducted by a juge d'instruction in serious criminal matters (crimes and complex délits). The juge d'instruction gathers evidence, interviews witnesses, and issues a mise en examen. Ends with a renvoi en jugement or a non-lieu.

---

## J

**Jugement** / *Judgment (first instance)* — A decision rendered by a first-instance court (tribunal judiciaire, tribunal de commerce, conseil de prud'hommes, etc.). Distinguished from arrêt (collegial appellate/cassation court) and ordonnance (single judge).

**Jurisprudence** / *Case law (jurisprudence)* — The body of court decisions interpreting and applying the law. In France, case law is not formally a source of law (courts cannot make binding rules — art. 5 Code civil), but a consistent line of decisions (jurisprudence constante) has strong de facto authority.

**Juge d'instruction** / *Investigating judge* — A specialized judicial officer (magistrat du siège) who independently investigates serious criminal matters before trial. Has broad powers: interrogations, searches, wiretaps, detention orders. Increasingly rare (only ~3% of criminal cases go through instruction).

---

## L

**Légitime défense** / *Self-defense* — A ground for irresponsibility in criminal law (art. 122-5 Code pénal): the use of force is justified if it is necessary, simultaneous with an unjustified attack, and proportionate to the seriousness of that attack. Similar concept applies to defense of property (art. 122-6).

**Lien de causalité** / *Causal link* — The required connection between the fault (or the thing/act) and the damage. In French tort law: assessed using the equivalence of conditions theory (théorie de l'équivalence des conditions) or, for multiple causes, proximate cause (théorie de la cause adéquate).

**Loi** / *Act of Parliament / Statute* — A text voted by Parliament (Assemblée nationale and Sénat). France operates under a restricted parliamentary domain (art. 34 Constitution); matters outside that domain are governed by décret.

**Loi de police** / *Mandatory rule (loi de police)* — A provision so fundamental to a legal order that it applies regardless of the law otherwise governing the contract or situation (private international law concept; art. 9 Rome I Regulation).

---

## M

**Mandat** / *Mandate / Agency* — A contract by which one party (the mandant) authorizes another (the mandataire) to act on their behalf (art. 1984 Code civil). Creates agency: acts of the mandataire bind the mandant.

**Mise en demeure** / *Formal notice* — A written demand requiring a party to perform an obligation within a specified time, failing which the creditor will resort to legal remedies. Required to start the running of interest (art. 1231-6 Code civil) and to claim damages for non-performance.

**Mise en examen** / *Being charged / Formally placed under investigation* — The formal notification by the juge d'instruction to a person that they are under serious suspicion of having committed an offence, giving them the rights of a party in the investigation (access to file, right to counsel). Not a conviction.

**Morale** / *Élément moral* — See: **Élément moral**.

---

## N

**Non bis in idem** / *Double jeopardy (non bis in idem)* — The principle that a person cannot be tried or punished twice for the same facts. Enshrined in art. 4 Protocol 7 ECHR and art. 50 EU Charter of Fundamental Rights.

**Non-lieu** / *No grounds for prosecution (non-lieu)* — An ordonnance de non-lieu is a decision by the juge d'instruction ending the investigation without sending the case to trial, because there is insufficient evidence or the acts do not constitute an offence.

**Notaire** / *Notary (notaire)* — A ministerial officer (officier ministériel) and legal professional who drafts and authenticates legal documents (actes authentiques) including real estate sales, wills, marriage contracts, and company formations.

**Nullité** / *Nullity* — The sanction for invalidity of a legal act. Nullité absolue (absolute nullity) for breach of public interest rules; can be raised by anyone. Nullité relative (relative nullity) for protection of a party's private interest (e.g., vices du consentement); can only be raised by the protected party.

---

## O

**Obligation de moyens** / *Best-efforts obligation* — An obligation in which the debtor must use all reasonable means to achieve a result but does not guarantee achieving it (e.g., a doctor's obligation to treat a patient). Breach requires proof of failure to exercise reasonable care.

**Obligation de résultat** / *Obligation of result* — An obligation in which the debtor guarantees achieving a specified outcome (e.g., a carrier delivering goods undamaged). Any failure to achieve the result constitutes a breach, without need to prove fault.

**Ordonnance** / *Order / Ordinance* — (1) A judicial decision by a single judge (e.g., juge des référés, juge d'instruction). (2) A legislative text enacted by the government under delegation from Parliament (art. 38 Constitution).

---

## P

**Parquet** / *Public prosecutor's office (Ministère public)* — The body of magistrats du parquet (procureurs) who represent society in criminal proceedings. Head: the Procureur de la République at first instance; Procureur général at the Court of Appeal; Procureur général près la Cour de cassation.

**Peine** / *Sentence / Penalty* — The criminal sanction imposed upon conviction. Main penalties: emprisonnement (imprisonment), travail d'intérêt général (community service), amende (fine), interdiction of rights, dissolution of legal entity.

**Personnalité morale** / *Legal personality (of an entity)* — The capacity of an entity (company, association, public body) to hold rights and obligations, sue and be sued. Acquired for commercial companies upon registration in the Registre du commerce et des sociétés (RCS).

**Pourvoi en cassation** / *Cassation appeal* — The extraordinary remedy by which a party challenges a final appellate decision before the Cour de cassation (civil/criminal) or the Conseil d'État (administrative), on grounds of misapplication of law. Does not re-examine facts. Time limit: 2 months in civil matters.

**Préjudice** / *Loss / Harm* — The damage that must be proven for a liability claim to succeed. Must be certain, direct, and personal. Categories: préjudice matériel (economic loss), préjudice moral (non-economic loss), préjudice corporel (bodily harm). Compensation aims at réparation intégrale.

**Prescription extinctive** / *Statute of limitations* — The extinction of a right to bring legal action after the expiry of a statutory period. General period: 5 years (art. 2224 Code civil) running from when the claimant knew or should have known the facts. Special periods: 10 years for bodily injury (art. 2226), 30 years for real property.

**Procureur de la République** / *Public Prosecutor* — The head of the parquet at a tribunal judiciaire. Decides whether to prosecute (opportunity principle), directs police in investigations, and represents the public interest in court.

**Promesse unilatérale** / *Unilateral promise* — A contract by which one party (the promisor) grants the other (the beneficiary) an option to conclude a future contract on pre-agreed terms (art. 1124 Code civil). Revocation before exercise of the option gives rise to damages, not forced completion (post-2016 reform: completion possible).

---

## Q

**Question prioritaire de constitutionnalité (QPC)** / *Priority preliminary ruling on constitutionality* — A mechanism introduced in 2010 (art. 61-1 Constitution) allowing any party in ongoing litigation to challenge the constitutionality of a statutory provision. The Cour de cassation or Conseil d'État filters; the Conseil constitutionnel decides within 3 months.

---

## R

**Référé** / *Summary / Interim proceedings* — An emergency judicial procedure decided by a single judge (juge des référés). Orders are provisional (not res judicata). Types include: référé d'urgence, référé provision (ordering payment on account), référé injonction de faire. Administrative equivalent: référé-suspension and référé-liberté.

**Régime matrimonial** / *Matrimonial property regime* — The legal framework governing ownership and management of assets between spouses. Default regime in France: communauté réduite aux acquêts (community of post-marital acquisitions). Alternatives include séparation de biens and communauté universelle.

**Réparation intégrale** / *Full compensation* — The foundational principle of French tort law: the victim must be placed in the position they would have been in had the harm not occurred. Neither more nor less. Punitive damages are not available in ordinary tort law.

**Responsabilité civile** / *Civil liability* — The obligation to compensate a victim for damage. Divided into contractual liability (responsabilité contractuelle, arts. 1231–1231-7 Code civil) and extra-contractual/delictual liability (responsabilité délictuelle, arts. 1240–1244 Code civil).

**Responsabilité pénale** / *Criminal liability* — The obligation of an individual or legal person to answer before criminal courts for an offence. Requires: a legal text defining the offence (légalité), a material element (acte), and a mental element (élément moral / mens rea).

**Rupture conventionnelle** / *Negotiated termination of employment* — A contractual exit from an open-ended employment contract (CDI), agreed between employer and employee, subject to administrative homologation (art. L. 1237-11 et seq. Code du travail). The employee receives severance and unemployment benefits.

---

## S

**Saisie** / *Attachment / Seizure* — A legal procedure enabling a creditor holding an enforceable title to seize a debtor's assets (bank accounts, movables, real property) to satisfy the debt. Governed by loi n° 91-650 of 9 July 1991.

**Solidarité** / *Joint and several liability (solidarité)* — An obligation mode where each co-debtor is liable for the entire debt (solidarité passive) or each co-creditor can demand the entire payment (solidarité active). Not presumed in civil law; must be stipulated or provided by statute (art. 1310 Code civil). Presumed between co-traders in commercial law.

**Sursis** / *Suspended sentence* — A criminal penalty the enforcement of which is suspended, conditionally (sursis avec mise à l'épreuve / sursis probatoire) or simply (sursis simple). If the condition of no re-offending (or probation terms) is met, the sentence is not executed.

---

## T

**Tribunal administratif** / *Administrative court (first instance)* — The first-instance court in the administrative judicial order, hearing recours against administrative decisions (annulment and full jurisdiction). Appeals go to the cour administrative d'appel, then to the Conseil d'État.

**Tribunal correctionnel** / *Criminal court for misdemeanors* — The division of the tribunal judiciaire that hears délits (misdemeanor-level offences). Composed of professional judges.

**Tribunal de commerce** / *Commercial court* — A first-instance court with jurisdiction over commercial disputes between traders, corporate law matters, and collective insolvency proceedings. Composed of elected commercial judges (juges consulaires — non-professional).

**Tribunal judiciaire** / *Civil and criminal court (first instance)* — Created in 2020 by merger of the tribunal de grande instance and tribunal d'instance. First-instance court for civil matters not attributed to specialist courts, and the tribunal correctionnel chamber for délits.

---

## U

**Usufruit** / *Usufruct* — A real property right giving its holder (usufruitier) the right to use and enjoy the fruits of an asset owned by another (nu-propriétaire), without being able to dispose of it (art. 578 Code civil). Commonly arises in inheritance or tax planning.

---

## V

**Vice caché** / *Hidden defect* — A defect in the goods sold that: was present at the time of sale, was hidden (not apparent on reasonable inspection), makes the goods unfit for their intended use or substantially reduces that use, and the buyer did not know of it. The seller's warranty is mandatory (art. 1641 Code civil).

**Vice du consentement** / *Defect of consent* — A flaw in the consent of a contracting party that renders the contract voidable (nullité relative). Three vices: erreur (mistake), dol (fraud/deceit), violence (duress) — arts. 1130–1143 Code civil.

**Violence** / *Duress (in contract law)* — A vice du consentement: one party's consent was obtained through illegitimate pressure (physical or economic) by the other party or a third party, leaving no reasonable alternative (art. 1140 Code civil). Economic duress (violence économique) is recognized under art. 1143.

---

## Z

**Zone de compétence exclusive** / *Exclusive jurisdiction* — A rule of private international law or procedural law designating only one court as competent over a dispute, regardless of party choice. Example: EU Regulation Brussels I bis art. 24 on real property, company seats, IP registrations.

---
